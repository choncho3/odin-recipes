Carbonara

Description:

This is a typical dish of the Lazio region of Italy. It's hot pasta tossed with a creamy sauce of raw beaten eggs, accentuated with crisp bits of guanciale, and finished with a shower of grated aged Pecorino Romano cheese plus freshly ground black pepper.


Ingredients:

  • 4 egg yolks
  • 50g pecorino Romano (Finely grated)
  • 20g pepper (Freshly cracked)
  • 100g guanciale (Diced)
  • 200g spaghetti

Steps:

  1. Boil water and add spaghetti
  2. Remove rind from guanciale and cut into 1/2 inch cubes
  3. Add cubes into cold pan and bring up the heat slowly
  4. While the guanciale begins to crsip, begin to whisk together the egg yolks, percorino romano, and pepper in a stainless bowl then set aside
  5. When the guanciale is crispy remove pieces from the pan and set aside. Make sureto keep the fat in the pan and keep warm!
  6. Create a double boiler by placing the stainless bowl with the egg mix over the boiling spaghetti. Place wooden spoon between the bowl and boiling pot to prevent it from getting too hot.
  7. Whisk until it begins to come together then add a small amount of pasta water.
  8. Slowly whisk 3/4 of the reserved guanciale fat
  9. When spaghetti is ready place into stainless bowl and toss over double boiler. Add pasta water if the sauce is too thick
  10. Add in the pieces of guanciale
  11. Serve with fresh cracked pepper and pecorino romano on top
